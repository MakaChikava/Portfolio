    I'm Makanaka Chikava, a software engineer with an unconventional path: from college and semi-pro soccer to Health and Sports Psychology, and now to tech. That journey wasn't linear, but every step of it shaped the way I think and work. Athletics taught me discipline and how to perform under pressure. Studying psychology deepened my understanding of people — how they think, what they need, and how good design can genuinely improve their experience.

    When I discovered coding, it felt like a natural convergence of everything I'd been building toward. I completed a coding bootcamp, where I sharpened my skills in full-stack development and got hands-on with the tools and practices the industry actually uses. Since then I've kept building — real projects, real problems, real users.

    What drives me is the intersection of technology and human experience. I care about writing clean, maintainable code, but I care just as much about the person on the other side of the screen. My diverse background gives me a perspective that's hard to come by in tech — I bring a team-first mindset, an eye for user-centered design, and an unwavering commitment to growth in everything I take on.
